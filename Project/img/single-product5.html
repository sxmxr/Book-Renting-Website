This novel is an allegory - even though it is set on a farm and stars a cast of farm animals, it reflects the events of the Russian revolution of 1917. The animals are all clever representations of Russian politicians, voters and workers.

Animal Farm is a novel about a group of animals who take control of the farm they live on. The animals get fed up of their master, Farmer Jones, so they kick him out.Animal Farm, known at the beginning and the end of the novel as the Manor Farm, symbolizes Russia and the Soviet Union under Communist Party rule. But more generally, Animal Farm stands for any human society, be it capitalist, socialist, fascist, or communist.

The main point of Animal Farm, by George Orwell, is to criticize communism, or at least the way that communism was implemented in the Soviet Union. The book shows how the communist movement starts with talk about helping the people and about letting everyone have a voice in their own lives.

I decided to read Animal Farm 1st as it was the smallest one and I wasn't disappointed. It's a serious topic discussed in a way that is simple to read and understand. Pretty much my favorite book. Me and some friends initially picked it up in english class as a joke but fell completely entranced by it when we finished.
